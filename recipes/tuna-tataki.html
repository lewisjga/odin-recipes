Tuna Tataki

Description

A simple, tuna-focused entree, this dish is little more than dressed up seared tuna. However, the tuna and its accompanying sauce and toppings make for a dish far better than its simplicity would let on. Make sure to get the freshest tuna you can to improve the experience even more.

  • 1/2 lb Tuna Steak
  • 3 oz Edamame
  • 1 tsp Ginger (Grated)
  • 3 tsp Sesame Oil
  • 2 tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 1 tbsp Water
  • 1 tbsp Rice Wine Vinegar
  • 1 1/2 tsp Sugar

Steps

  1. Brush tuna with 2 tsp of your sesame oil and season well
  2. Heat a frying pan on medium-high heat, and cook the tuna for 30 seconds on each side, searing but leaving the inside rare
  3. Rinse tuna in cold water, then pat dry and slice thinly
  4. Blanch the edamame in lightly salted boiling water for one minute, drain, rinse in cold water, and pat dry
  5. Set aside tuna and edamame, then combine all remaining ingredients in a bowl to create the sauce
  6. Plate the tuna, and garnish with edamame, drizzle sauce on top and serve
